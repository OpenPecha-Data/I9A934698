PREFACE
A-’dzom 'Brug-pa Sprul-sku 'Gro-’dul-dpa’-bo-rdo-rje was
one of the greatest visionaries and revealers of concealed teachings
of the last century. He belonged to the post-Mkhyen-brtse and
Kong-sprul generation. According to Dhongthog Rimpoche (Important
events in Tibetan history, n. 113), he was born in 1842, although this
dating is far from definitive. He was regarded as an incarnation of
the Rgval-dbang 'Brug-paline of presiding lamas of the 'Brug-pa
Dkar-brgyud-pa sect (Northern branch) According to the Ven.' Brug
pa Thugs-sras Rin-po-che, the son of the legitimist 10th Rgyal-dban
'Brug-pa 'Jigs-med-mi-pham-chos-dbang of Gsang-sngags-chos-gling, he
passed away about 1924 He was never recognized as the 'Brug-pa
hierarch, although he had many followers in Khams. His seat of
residence was A-’dzom 'Bring-na Chos-sgar. He was very active
and is responsible for the blocks of numerous Rnying-ma-pa liturgico-
meditational collections. The prints of A-'dzom 'Brug-pa Chos-sgar
enjoyed a considerable reputation for their reliability and beauty.
The collection reproduced in this volume focusses upon Rdo-
rje-gro-lod, an aspect of the Mahasiddha Padmasambhava. The Rdo-
rje-gro-lod belong to the Gu-ru Drag-po (or Bla-ma Drag-po) class
of teachings very popular in Khams. The original print belonging
to Dudjom Rimpoche was found to be not suitable for reprinting.
It was necessary to resort to recopying the water-soaked original
A-'dzom ’Brug-pa Chos-sgar print.
